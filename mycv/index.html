Projects

Iron Mind Exoskeleton

The Iron Mind Exoskeleton is an innovative wearable robotic system designed to assist and enhance human physical movement. This semi-automated exoskeleton is intended primarily for individuals who face mobility challenges due to injury, age, or disability, as well as for workers in physically demanding environments. The suit functions by integrating motor-assisted joints and sensors that detect the user's movement intentions, providing support and strength to the limbs during motion. Built using lightweight materials and powered by servo motors, the system uses a microcontroller such as Arduino to process real-time data from motion sensors and control the actuators accordingly. The Iron Mind Exoskeleton aims to reduce muscle strain, improve mobility, and assist in rehabilitation for patients recovering from strokes or injuries. This project highlights the use of embedded systems, biomechanics, and control systems to create a functional and user-friendly wearable device. It has significant applications in medical rehabilitation, industrial safety, and defense. The prototype developed demonstrated successful assisted limb movement, earning recognition and a gold medal at the Talentronics Project Fest.
